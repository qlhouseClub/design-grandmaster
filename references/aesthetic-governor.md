## 1. Activation and ambition

Do not interpret “high-end” as more effects. Declare the required finish:

| Level | Standard |
|---|---|
| Correct | Legible, usable, consistent, appropriate |
| Polished | Refined spacing, type, imagery, states, and implementation |
| Distinctive | Recognizable concept and non-generic visual grammar |
| Authored | Clear point of view; choices feel intentionally composed rather than assembled |
| Inevitable | Form, brand, content, and interaction feel inseparable; removing a choice weakens the whole |

Default high-aesthetic requests to **Authored**. Reserve **Inevitable** for identity-defining launches, flagship experiences, cultural/editorial work, or when the user explicitly demands the highest bar.

Never claim a level without evidence from the craft and stress-test passes.

## 2. Reference intelligence

For high-aesthetic, premium, historical, cultural, trend-led, or unfamiliar visual work, complete client taste discovery and visual research before directing form. Read [aesthetic-discovery-research.md](aesthetic-discovery-research.md) through the routing in `SKILL.md`; do not let this governor score directions built on an unsourced moodboard.

Use browsing, image search, institutional collections, scholarship, supplied artifacts, and visual files as appropriate. Research must be broad enough to reveal contradictions, not only references that confirm the first idea.

Build a board from distinct lanes:

- **Lineage:** historical movement or period that explains the visual logic
- **Category:** current peers and audience expectations
- **Adjacency:** architecture, fashion, publishing, industrial design, film, art, games, or music with transferable principles
- **Counter-reference:** what the work must not resemble
- **Material/interaction:** real examples of type, image, motion, surface, or spatial behavior

Use enough references to reveal a field, not one reference to clone. Prefer several sources across at least three lanes after the fuller discovery corpus has been built. Record provenance and date; distinguish original artifact, reproduction, restoration, later revival, and contemporary interpretation.

Deconstruct references by mechanism:

| Reference | Composition | Type | Color/light | Material/texture | Image | Motion | Emotion | Transferable principle | Do not copy |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Avoid “clean,” “premium,” “bold,” or “modern” without visual mechanics. Explain what produces the effect.

### Reference distance

- Borrow a principle from lineage.
- Borrow a technique from an adjacent field.
- Respect one or two category conventions needed for comprehension.
- Add a product/brand-specific mutation.
- Reject any direction whose identity depends on one recognizable reference.

## 3. Aesthetic dials

Set reasoned bands, not false-precision numbers:

- **Order:** rigorous grid / controlled irregularity / deliberate chaos
- **Expression:** restrained / articulate / exuberant
- **Familiarity:** conventional / reframed / alien
- **Materiality:** flat / layered / tactile / optical-spatial
- **Motion:** static / responsive / choreographed / cinematic
- **Density:** gallery / editorial / application / cockpit
- **Human trace:** engineered / softened / handmade / raw
- **Temporal stance:** timeless / period-rooted / contemporary / emerging / speculative
- **Brand specificity:** category-fit / distinctive / unmistakable

For every dial state the reason, the risk at one step higher, and the minimum viable expression at one step lower. This creates a useful operating range instead of a mood-setting number.

## 4. Competing directions

For high-aesthetic work, default to three materially different art directions and include one less-obvious direction when the brand can sustain it. Collapse to two only when a strong approved baseline narrows the question to one meaningful trade-off; use one only for low-risk conformance where there is no unresolved visual direction. Do not turn the direction count into ceremony.

Each direction must contain:

1. Thesis in one sentence
2. Anti-thesis: what it refuses to become
3. Lineage and contemporary mutation
4. Audience and emotional fit
5. Composition, typography, color/light, material, image, icon, motion, and voice grammar
6. One or two signature moves
7. What remains deliberately plain
8. Representative hero/key moment and mundane utility state
9. System extension across at least three different surfaces
10. Accessibility, performance, production, and trend-expiry risks
11. Why it could fail

Do not create three colorways of one layout. If the silhouettes are nearly identical, divergence failed.

## 4A. Representative proofs and approval gate

When the choice depends on visual judgment, prove each direction with comparable artifacts before the jury:

- Use the same content, asset set, viewport/frame, and functional requirement.
- Show a signature moment plus an ordinary utility state where the system must survive without spectacle.
- For a deck, use at least two structurally different representative slides; for motion, use key moments and transition logic; for a full application, use the critical path and one recovery or dense state.
- Compare silhouette, value structure, type voice, palette topology, material, image treatment, motion character, and restraint—not only preference.
- Record the selected direction, owner, rejected alternatives, permitted mixing, and revisit trigger when the decision is expensive or multi-stakeholder.

If rendering is unavailable, label the direction as specified but visually unproven. Prose cannot establish `Authored` or `Inevitable` finish.

## 5. The aesthetic jury

Apply non-compensatory gates first:

- Task and hierarchy remain understandable
- Brand and audience fit are credible
- Accessibility and cultural integrity are viable
- Real content and required states work
- Platform and production constraints are respected

A failure in a gate rejects the direction. Spectacle elsewhere cannot compensate.

Compare surviving directions pairwise using:

- **Inevitability:** does form feel derived from this exact purpose and content?
- **Distinctiveness:** is it recognizable among peers without relying on logo or novelty garnish?
- **Emotional precision:** does it create the intended feeling at the intended moment?
- **Compositional tension:** is there controlled contrast, rhythm, and surprise rather than bland balance or random disruption?
- **Craft depth:** do typography, spacing, imagery, material, and motion reward close inspection?
- **System fertility:** can the idea generate many coherent surfaces without repetition?
- **Temporal intelligence:** does it understand its lineage, contemporary meaning, saturation, and likely half-life?
- **Restraint:** does the design know where to stop?
- **Memorability:** can a viewer describe the idea later in one concrete sentence?

Use `weak / viable / strong / exceptional` with rationale. Do not total scores mechanically. State the decisive trade-off and why the winner is worth its risks.

## 6. Craft loop

Refine in separate passes. Do not polish everything at once.

### Pass 1: Silhouette and hierarchy

Blur or squint at the composition. The focal point, sequence, mass, and whitespace must read before color and detail.

### Pass 2: Typography as composition

Tune type roles, scale contrast, line breaks, measure, leading, rhythm, optical alignment, numerals, and script coverage. Treat headline shape as part of the composition without damaging language.

### Pass 3: Spatial rhythm

Audit alignment lines, negative space, density transitions, repetition, interruption, edge relationships, and responsive re-composition. Perfectly equal spacing is not automatically good rhythm.

### Pass 4: Color, light, and material

Check value structure in grayscale first. Then tune hue, temperature, saturation, depth, reflection, texture, translucency, and contrast. Every material effect must describe layer, interaction, or atmosphere.

### Pass 5: Imagery and iconography

Demand coherent art direction, crop, light, perspective, subject, texture, and provenance. Reject assets that merely fill space or create a false product preview.

### Pass 6: Motion and time

Define entrance, response, transition, continuity, and rest. Motion must improve causality, hierarchy, emotion, or spatial understanding. Design the still frame and reduced-motion experience first.

### Pass 7: Micro-detail

Tune borders, radii, dividers, shadows, highlights, focus, hover, loading, truncation, empty/error states, and optical corrections. Micro-detail may reinforce the thesis but must not introduce a second style.

### Pass 8: System lineup

Review key surfaces together as a contact sheet. Check whether they look like one world without becoming the same composition repeated.

### Pass 9: Subtraction

Remove decorative moves one by one. Keep only those whose removal reduces meaning, hierarchy, emotion, or identity.

## 7. Visual stress tests

- **Three-second test:** can someone identify purpose, focal point, and emotional register?
- **Squint/blur test:** does hierarchy survive detail loss?
- **Grayscale test:** does structure work without hue?
- **Logo-off test:** is the brand still recognizable?
- **Content stress:** short, long, empty, dense, localized, and error content remain composed?
- **Mundane-screen test:** does the visual thesis survive settings, forms, tables, and recovery states?
- **Motion-off test:** does meaning survive static and reduced-motion modes?
- **Peer lineup test:** does it separate from five category peers for a meaningful reason?
- **Trend half-life test:** will the work retain value after the fashionable signature saturates?
- **Craft zoom:** do close details reveal intention rather than inconsistency?
- **Thumbnail/color-anchor test:** at reduced size, does the focal hierarchy and intended chromatic anchor survive without every hue becoming equally loud?
- **Negative-space test:** does open space create scale, rhythm, calm, suspense, or interaction room, or is it merely unused area?

## 8. Anti-convergence rules

- Do not equate minimalism with large type, pale gray, and empty space.
- Do not equate premium with black, beige, serif, glass, or slow motion.
- Do not equate technology with neon, dark mesh, monospace, grids, or fake telemetry.
- Do not equate playfulness with blobs, stickers, gradients, or bouncing icons.
- Do not equate editorial with random serif/sans mixing and asymmetry.
- Do not equate contemporary with the latest platform material.
- Do not generate fake numbers, awards, testimonials, product screenshots, or cultural credibility as visual texture.
- Do not solve generic output by accumulating bans. Replace rejected defaults with a positive thesis and specific composition.
- Do not let every section compete. A visual system needs peaks, valleys, silence, and utility.

If the result can be summarized only as a trend label, it is not authored enough.

## 9. Output contract

For high-aesthetic work, include an **Aesthetic Decision Record**:

1. Ambition level and design intent
2. Reference matrix and counter-references
3. Temporal lineage and current signal date
4. Aesthetic dials and operating ranges
5. Competing directions and the approved adaptive count
6. Comparable representative proofs and direction approval record
7. Jury comparison and decisive trade-off
8. Chosen thesis and anti-thesis
9. Visual grammar, signature moves, and deliberate restraint
10. Craft passes and stress-test evidence
11. Trend expiry/revisit trigger
12. Remaining human art-direction decisions

If no visual inspection or rendering tool is available, provide an art-direction specification and state that the visual finish has not been proven. Do not claim `Authored` or `Inevitable` from prose alone.
