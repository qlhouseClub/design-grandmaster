# Visual Trend Atlas and Radar

This atlas is a starting taxonomy, not a closed style menu. Never choose from it before learning the client and researching the specific project field. The Liquid Glass section is one worked trend passport because platform semantics matter; it has no privileged status over other historical, cultural, emerging, regional, material, or counter-trend directions.

Use this module for period-aware art direction, historical references, contemporary trend work, and requests involving “current,” “recent,” “popular,” Liquid Glass, glassmorphism, expressive UI, retro styles, or future-facing aesthetics.

## 1. How to use a visual lineage

Treat a movement as a response to its technology, culture, economics, and media. Extract its principles before its surface signals. For actual art direction, use [aesthetic-discovery-research.md](aesthetic-discovery-research.md) through the routing in `SKILL.md` to build a live project-specific corpus; do not mistake this condensed table for research.

For any period or movement identify:

- Origin conditions and original medium
- Composition and grid
- Typography and image relationship
- Color, material, texture, and production marks
- Motion or temporal behavior when applicable
- Emotional and ideological register
- What the style communicates today, which may differ from its origin
- Suitable contexts and cultural risks
- Contemporary mutation and implementation constraints
- Saturation, nostalgia, and likely half-life

Use one primary lineage, optionally one counterpoint, and a product-specific mutation. Mixing many recognizable eras without a thesis becomes costume collage.

## 3. Trend adaptation method

Use this chain:

`lineage -> original problem -> enduring principle -> current signal -> brand/context mutation -> bounded signature -> system rule -> expiry test`

Example: do not translate Liquid Glass as “apply blur.” Translate it as “separate a responsive functional layer from content using dynamic optical material,” then decide whether that principle fits the platform and task.

Classify a trend's role:

- **Structural:** changes layout, hierarchy, navigation, or system logic
- **Material:** changes surface, light, texture, or depth
- **Typographic:** changes voice, variable axes, scale, or text behavior
- **Image:** changes photography, illustration, rendering, or collage
- **Temporal:** changes motion, transition, interaction, or narrative
- **Cultural:** changes representation, authorship, locality, or attitude

Limit a trend to the role it can support. A material trend should not silently reorganize information architecture.

## 4. Current trend radar

This snapshot was checked on **2026-07-22**. Reverify before claiming “current,” “latest,” or “recent.”

Signals visible across official platform work and major 2026 creative reports include:

- **Dynamic optical material:** Liquid Glass and related spatial/translucent layer systems
- **Emotion-led expressive UI:** color, shape, size, containment, variable type, and motion used to improve attention and emotional connection
- **Tactile imperfection and human trace:** texture, drawing, collage, analog marks, and resistance to synthetic perfection
- **Structured simplicity:** reduced noise and clearer hierarchy as a countercurrent to expression
- **Reality distortion and sensory surrealism:** warped imagery, spatial scenes, blur, transformation, and rich compositing
- **Local and culturally specific visual language:** movement away from globally interchangeable template aesthetics
- **Purposeful motion and layered storytelling:** motion as exploration, reveal, and spatial narrative rather than generic entrance animation

Treat trend reports as cultural signals, not instructions. Triangulate with production interfaces, platform guidance, search/image evidence, and the target audience.

### Adoption stage

Label each candidate trend:

- Signal: early evidence, unstable vocabulary
- Emerging: growing production adoption
- Peak: widely recognized and commercially useful
- Saturated: high template risk
- Backlash: counter-trend is gaining energy
- Nostalgic return: old grammar with new cultural meaning
- Evergreen: principle has outlived its original style cycle

State the evidence date and what would make the classification change.

## 5. Liquid Glass passport

### Origin and official meaning

Apple introduced Liquid Glass in 2025 as a cross-platform dynamic material for iOS 26, iPadOS 26, macOS Tahoe 26, watchOS 26, and tvOS 26. Official guidance describes optical glass qualities plus fluidity, adaptive response to content/context, and a distinct **functional layer** for navigation and controls above content.

### Native Apple rules

- Prefer current SwiftUI, UIKit, and AppKit system components; standard bars, sheets, popovers, and controls adopt the material and adaptive behavior.
- Use Liquid Glass primarily for navigation and controls, not as the content-layer background.
- Use custom glass sparingly and reserve it for important functional elements.
- Choose `regular` when text/background complexity requires stronger legibility; choose `clear` over visually rich media only when contrast remains sufficient.
- Remove custom backgrounds that interfere with system-provided material and scroll-edge behavior.
- Test light/dark environments, overlap, focus, reduced transparency, increased contrast, and reduced motion.
- Respect platform-specific navigation, window, sheet, toolbar, focus, and performance behavior.

### Web and non-Apple translation

Call the result **Liquid-Glass-inspired** or **dynamic glass material**, not official Apple Liquid Glass.

Build a full optical stack rather than blur alone:

- Underlying content with meaningful parallax or movement
- Adaptive translucent fill and tint
- Backdrop blur/saturation only where performance allows
- Edge refraction through inner highlights and layered borders
- Controlled specular response to interaction
- Shape continuity or morphing tied to navigation/state
- Strong foreground contrast and dimming/scrim when needed
- Solid-material fallback for unsupported or reduced-transparency contexts
- Reduced-motion and low-power behavior

Use it as a functional top layer: floating navigation, media controls, transient toolbars, contextual actions, or immersive overlays. Avoid applying it to every card, long-form content, dense data tables, or critical forms.

### Fit test

Strong fit:

- Apple-native applications adopting current platform conventions
- Media-rich, spatial, premium-consumer, entertainment, or device-adjacent experiences
- Interfaces where content benefits from remaining visible beneath navigation/control layers
- Brands whose promise includes fluidity, light, depth, or adaptive technology

Weak fit:

- Data-dense operations where optical backgrounds compete with scanning
- Low-power or performance-constrained environments without graceful fallback
- Trust contexts where transparency reduces legibility or perceived solidity
- Brands built on paper, raw utility, industrial rigidity, or historical material honesty unless deliberately contrasted
- Any design using glass solely to look current

### Distinctive mutation

Do not reproduce Apple's exact pills and navigation silhouette. Change at least the material behavior, geometry, lighting model, motion character, typography relationship, or cultural reference so the system belongs to the product.

### Failure signatures

- Blur plus white border presented as Liquid Glass
- Glass on content, navigation, cards, inputs, and modals simultaneously
- Low-contrast labels over uncontrolled imagery
- Random blobs or shine disconnected from state
- Heavy rendering with no performance plan
- No reduced-transparency or reduced-motion fallback
- Apple mimicry on a non-Apple brand with no rationale

## 7. Live-source protocol

When the request depends on current taste:

1. Search sources from the last 12-18 months.
2. Prefer official platform/design-system guidance for named materials or systems.
3. Inspect current production work and image evidence, not trend articles alone.
4. Compare at least two independent trend reports for broad cultural claims.
5. Separate reported trend, observed adoption, and your inference.
6. Record publication/event date and the date checked.
7. State whether the signal is platform-native, cross-platform, web approximation, or purely visual culture.
8. Preserve direct sources in the final art-direction record.

Never claim a style is current from memory alone.
