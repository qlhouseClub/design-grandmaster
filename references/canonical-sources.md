# Canonical Sources

Use these sources when current standards, exact pattern behavior, or implementation examples matter. Prefer them over summaries or visual imitation.

## Atomic design

- Brad Frost, Atomic Design methodology: https://atomicdesign.bradfrost.com/chapter-2/
- Use atoms, molecules, organisms, templates, and pages as concurrent views of a system, not a mandatory linear process or repository taxonomy.

## Accessibility

- W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2: https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/
- W3C WAI-ARIA Authoring Practices Guide: https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/
- APG patterns include roles, states, keyboard interaction, and functional examples. A visual pattern without its full interaction contract is not the pattern.

## Open-source design system reference

- GitHub Primer design system: https://primer.github.io/design/
- Primer organization and repositories: https://github.com/primer
- Use Primer as evidence for how foundations, primitives/tokens, components, patterns, accessibility, Figma, code, lifecycle, and contribution can coexist. Do not copy GitHub's visual language when the product's brand and audience call for another system.

## Contemporary platform and material systems

- Apple announcement of Liquid Glass, June 2025: https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2025/06/apple-introduces-a-delightful-and-elegant-new-software-design/
- Apple HIG materials and Liquid Glass: https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/materials
- Apple adoption guidance: https://developer.apple.com/documentation/TechnologyOverviews/adopting-liquid-glass
- Google research behind Material 3 Expressive: https://design.google/library/expressive-material-design-google-research
- Google Material design eras: https://design.google/library/material-design-eras
- Microsoft Fluent Acrylic material: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/apps/design/style/acrylic

Use official platform semantics before borrowing appearance. Liquid Glass is an Apple platform material, Material 3 Expressive is a researched system of emotional and functional expression, and Acrylic has its own Windows role. Their visual similarities do not make them interchangeable.

## Current creative-trend signals

- Canva Design Trends 2026: https://www.canva.com/design-trends/
- Adobe Creative Trends 2026: https://business.adobe.com/resources/creative-trends-report.html

Treat commercial trend reports as broad cultural signals, not design requirements. Triangulate them with current production work, image evidence, platform guidance, audience research, and the brand's own history.

## Historical and cultural visual research

Use a source hierarchy rather than a generic image search:

1. Object records, archives, archaeological reports, digitized originals, and in-situ documentation
2. Museums, heritage institutes, conservation labs, and curated exhibitions
3. Peer-reviewed historical, material, pigment, craft, and conservation research
4. Named practitioners and documented production processes
5. Contemporary production examples, criticism, and audience reception

For every consequential visual source, record provenance, object/publication date, access date, original/restored/reconstructed/reproduced status, and rights. Search-result thumbnails, reposts, Pinterest-style boards, AI image feeds, and unsourced color palettes are discovery leads, not historical evidence.

When a modern term is projected onto a historical period, research the term and the historical objects separately. Never let one surviving site, social class, region, reconstruction, or contemporary revival stand in for an entire culture.

## Source discipline

- Verify version-sensitive guidance at the canonical URL before asserting it is current.
- For “current/latest/recent” visual-trend claims, browse live and record both source date and check date.
- Use official platform systems for platform-specific behavior when applicable.
- Treat examples as implementation evidence, not universal aesthetic defaults.
- Separate sourced fact, direct visual observation, inference, and proposed translation.
- Preserve source URLs in audits or specifications when a recommendation depends on a specific criterion or pattern.
