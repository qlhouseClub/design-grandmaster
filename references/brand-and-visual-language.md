# Brand and Visual Language

Use this module for brand extraction, visual direction, art direction, typography, color, imagery, iconography, and consistent expression.

## Extract brand truth

Start from real shipped evidence: product, site, packaging, campaigns, support, documents, logo assets, and copy. Record what is observed versus inferred.

Extract:

- Promise and positioning expressed through behavior
- Audience and category expectations
- Recognizable assets and signature moves
- Color roles and usage proportions
- Typography hierarchy, voice, rhythm, and language coverage
- Spacing, density, shape, border, elevation, materiality
- Image/illustration/icon treatment
- Motion and sound character when relevant
- Verbal voice, terminology, and tone by context
- Inconsistencies to resolve and equity to preserve

Do not guess a famous brand's values from memory. Do not infer a usable visual system from a mission statement alone.

## Direction exploration

Define two or three distinct concepts with axes, not mood-board adjectives:

| Dimension | Direction A | Direction B |
|---|---|---|
| Familiarity | conventional | category-breaking |
| Energy | calm | dynamic |
| Density | spacious | information-rich |
| Voice | technical | conversational |
| Material | flat/graphic | tactile/photographic |
| Geometry | precise | organic |

For each concept specify:

- One-sentence idea and audience fit
- Desired emotional effect
- Typography, color, grid, shape, image, icon, motion, and voice grammar
- Signature move and deliberate restraint
- Accessibility and implementation risks
- Why it could fail

Choose one direction and carry it through every design layer. Mixing favorite parts from incompatible concepts usually produces generic work.

## Typography system

Select type for voice, readability, language coverage, numerals, platform rendering, licensing, and performance.

Define:

- Primary/secondary/mono roles only when each has a purpose
- Responsive scale and semantic styles
- Weight, leading, tracking, measure, paragraph rhythm
- Heading length/case rules
- Numeral, table, code, caption, label, and link treatment
- CJK/Arabic/Devanagari or fallback strategy where relevant
- Loading and fallback metric compatibility for web

Default body measure near 45–75 characters depending on script and context. Validate dense interfaces, long localization, zoom, and real content. Do not use tiny/light text as a substitute for hierarchy.

## Color system

Define color by role:

- Canvas/surface/elevated surface
- Text primary/secondary/disabled/inverse
- Border/divider/focus
- Action primary/secondary/destructive
- Informational/success/warning/error
- Selection, data visualization, and brand accent

For each role provide value by theme/mode, contrast requirement, permitted contexts, and forbidden uses. Never rely on color alone for state. Preserve one accent logic unless the system explicitly needs categorical colors.

## Layout and spacing

- Choose a base rhythm and a bounded scale, not arbitrary values.
- Define page grids, content width, gutters, alignment lines, and density modes.
- Use proximity to express relationships and whitespace to separate concepts.
- Break the grid only to emphasize an intentional focal point.
- Preserve vertical rhythm across typography, components, and sections.

## Shape, surface, and material

Define radius, border, elevation, translucency, and texture as a grammar. Give each level a meaning. Avoid mixing sharp, soft, pill, glass, and heavy-shadow languages without explicit rules.

## Imagery and illustration

- Set subject, composition, crop, perspective, lighting, palette, texture, and human representation rules.
- Use real or purpose-made assets with known rights and honest provenance.
- Define alt-text intent and when an image is decorative.
- Avoid generic stock metaphors, fake product screenshots, and imagery that contradicts actual product capability.

## Iconography

Choose one family or define a controlled custom system. Specify grid, size, stroke/fill, optical alignment, corner treatment, color, labels, and accessible names. Icons supplement meaning; they do not replace unfamiliar labels.

## Voice and content expression

Translate brand adjectives into mechanics:

- Sentence length and syntax
- Person and agency
- Jargon and terminology
- Capitalization/punctuation
- Tone for onboarding, success, warning, error, high-stakes confirmation, and support
- Do/don't pairs using real copy

## Originality without novelty debt

- Make one or two signature moves memorable; keep routine controls familiar.
- Derive variation from brand and content, not current aesthetic defaults.
- Vary layout families when the narrative changes, while preserving the same grid, type, color, and shape grammar.
- Audit for template tells: repeated card rows, decorative gradients, meaningless pills, fake data, excessive center alignment, and motion without purpose.

Distinctiveness is a recognizable system, not constant surprise.
